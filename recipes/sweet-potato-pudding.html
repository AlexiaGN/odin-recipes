Jamaican Sweet Potato Pudding


Description

This classic Jamaican dessert is moist and rich with an indulgent taste that will have your mouth watering in seconds. The combination of sweet potato and coconut makes this Caribbean dessert one you will crave for often. This recipe from Serenalissy is a modified version of what is used in Jamaica.

Ingredients

  • A half cup unsweetened desiccated coconut
  • One and a half pound sweet potato
  • A half pound yam
  • 3 tablespoons of freshly grated ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground allspice
  • 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon
  • 2 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups cocnut milk
  • A quarter cup melted unsalted butter
  • A quarter cup cornmeal
  • A third cup all-purpose flour
  • One and a half cup sugar
  • 2 teaspoon salt
  • A half cup golden raisins soaked in 3 tableshpoons of dark rum overnight

Topping Ingredients

  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • A half teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • A half tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • A half cup coconut milk

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F. Grease a 10" springform pan.
  2. Rehydrate your cocunut. Soak in hot water for 15-20 minutes to rehydrate, then squeeze out all the water.
  3. Peel all the yams, sweet potatoes, and ginger. Set a box grater, zester side up, into a large mixing bowl. Grate the sweet potatoes, yam and ginger using the zester.
  4. Bring a cup of water to a boil in a small pot on medium high heat. Add in the allspice, nutmeg, cinnamon and vanilla and let boil for 2 minutes. Once this is done, pour the spice mixture into the sweet potato mixture along with the coconut milk and melted butter.
  5. Using an electric hand mixer, combine until homogenous.
  6. In a medium bowl, combine the rehydrated coconut, cornmeal, flour, sugar, and salt. Then slowly add to the wet mixture, continuing to use the electric mixer at medium speed until combined. Drain the raisins.
  7. Pour the mixture into the prepared pan and bake for one hour in the preheated oven. Remove from oven, add the topping, and then continue to cook for 15 more minutes.
  8. A wooden skewer inserted into the center should come out clean. You will see carmelization around the edges of the baking pan, but this will not be set like a cake. The consistency will be similar to that of pumpkin pie with a stiff filling.
  9. Let cool for 30 minutes, Use a knife around the edge of the pan and gently remove the side ring. Continue to let cool at room temperature on a wire rack for 2 hours or more, until cool completely. Gently remove the bottom of the springform pan.

Topping

  1. In a small saucepan boil your spice mixture. Combine all ingredients on medium high heat and bring to a boil. Continue to boil until reduced by half. (About 10 minutes). Remove from heat and let cool.
