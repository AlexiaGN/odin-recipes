Rum Cake


Description

Jamaican Black Cake, also known as rum cake, is a rich, dense, boozy Caribbean desset made of dried fruit soaked in rum, warm spices and wine. Whatever you call it, this black cake recipe by Seasoned Skillet is perfect for any holidaay, celebration or special occasion.

Ingredients

  • A half cup salted butter
  • A cup dark brown sugar
  • 6 large eggs
  • 2 tbsp vanilla extract
  • 5 tbsp browning
  • 2 tbsp molasses
  • 1 tbsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp ground nutmeg
  • A quarter tsp ground allspice
  • One and a half cup all purpose flour
  • One and a half tsp baking powder
  • A half tsp salt
  • 2 cups soaked fruit
  • A quarter cup Wray and Nephew White Overproof Rum
  • A quarter cup J. Wray and Nephew Red Label Wine
  • Juice from half of a lime
  • Zest from a half of a lime

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 300 degrees F and prepare a 9-inch round cake pan by greasing the bottom and sides using butter. Set aside an additional cake pan of water to be placed at the bottom of the oven during the baking process.
  2. Add the butter and dark brown sugar to a large mixing bowl or the bowl of a stand mixer and cream on medium speed until light and fluffy (about 5 to 6 minutes). Set aside.
  3. Add eggs to another large bowl, and beat using a hand mixer or whisk until light yellow in colour and foamy on top (about 3 to 5 minutes).
  4. To the egg mixture add the vanilla, browning, molasses, lime juice and gently mix using low speed (about 30 seconds). Then, add cinnamon, nutmeg, allspice, baking powder, salt, and lime zest and gently mix using low speed again (about 30 seconds).
  5. Pour egg mixture into the butter-sugar mixture, and fold well with a wooden spoon until fully mixed (about 3 minutes). Then fold in the pureed fruit mixture, and lastly the flour.
  6. Pour cake mixture into prepared cake pan and place into the oven along with the additional cake pan of water underneath (on the bottom rack) to ensure the cake does not dry out.
  7. Bake for approximately 2 hours (baking times will vary), cake is ready when a toothpick comes out clean or almost clean after inserting into the center of the cake.
  8. Once cake done, remove from oven and place cake pan on a cooling rack. Then, pour 1/2 cup of J. Wray & Nephew Red Label wine overtop while hot. Allow to soak into cake. Then when completely cooled, remove cake from pan, slice and enjoy!
